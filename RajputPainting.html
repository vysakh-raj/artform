Rajput paninting

Also called Rajasthani Painting, Rajput Painting is a style of Indian painting that evolved in the royal courts of Rajputana kings of Rajasthan in the late 16th and early 17th century.
Each Rajput kingdom displayed a distinct style of painting, though with some resemblance.
The two most common schools under the Rajput paintings were Rajasthani Paintings and Pahari Paintings; While Rajasthani school of paintings covered the areas of Rajasthan like Mewar (later Udaipur), Jaipur, Bundi, Kota, Kishangarh, Jodhpur, Bikaner etc.
On the other hand, the Pahari School of paintings covered the hilly regions like Basohli, Kangra, Guler, Nurpur, Chamba, Mandi, Haripur and Tehri-Garwal.
Since most of the princely states of Rajasthan were ruled by the Mughals at that time, the art schools of Rajput paintings reflected strong Mughal influence.
